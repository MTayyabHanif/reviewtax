What Is Tax Relief and Do These Tax Firms Listed Really Help?

Yes, in many cases having a tax professional on your side can make a difference on the outcome. Tax Relief is the process or helping taxpayers resolve tax problems with taxation authorities or tax agencies. Generally, the two main types of tax agencies that taxpayers face problems with include the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) and or state tax agencies.

Licensed tax professionals, such as tax attorneys, CPAs, and Enrolled Agents can work out agreements with the IRS or State on your behalf. The course of action that a tax professional will pursue depends on your tax situation, and your financial situation (assets, liabilities, monthly income and expenses. Here are some of the common tax options that tax professionals can assist with and pursue:

1) Payment Plan - This is generally whereby you would pay the IRS or state monthly until the balance has reached out including any penalties and interest.

2) Partial Payment Installment Agreement - If your assets, liabilities, and income match up you may qualify for a partial payment installment agreement, especially if an OIC didn't work out. With this type of IA, you may end up getting some of the tax amount forgiven.

3) Penalty Abatement - Generally this is coupled with a payment plan. Penalties that can be reduced or eliminated include the failure to file penalty, failure to pay penalty, and failure to deposit penalty (for companies).

4) Currently Not Collectible - If a taxpayer cannot make the monthly minimum payment on a payment plan, they can look to be put in a CNC status by IRS. Some states offer this but not it varies. If a taxpayer is in a CNC with the IRS, penalties and interest continue to accrue. However, if the time clock for collecting the taxes (generally 10 years with the IRS) expires, the balance can be forgiven.

5) OIC - This is a form of settlement whereby a taxpayer can pay less than they owe. However, not everyone qualifies. The taxpayer must be in a tough financial situation and make an offer to the IRS that the IRS believes is fair. Calculating the offer amount and completing the paperwork can be a cumbersome process and therefore a tax professional is recommended.

6) Innocent Spouse Relief - Sometimes one spouse may be held wrongly responsible for tax liabilities because the other spouse understated his or her taxes, failed to pay taxes on time, or failed to file on time among other things. In certain cases, if one spouse can prove they didn't know or didn't have any reason to know, certain states and the IRS may provide relief to him or her.

7) Filing Unfiled Tax Returns - Whether a business or individual, in most cases, a taxpayer will need to become compliant with the IRS or state before any type fo agreement is set up.

If you owe at least $10,000, it is generally worth it. You wouldn't go to court without an attorney or do surgery on yourself without a doctor right? Remember, the IRS and most states want to collect as much as possible and not always looking out in your best interests.

How To Find Out How Much I Owe?

If you are dealing with the IRS, you can check a recent letter from the IRS or call them at 1-800-829-1040. If you have a problem with a paritcular state, call the department of revenue phone number. Realize that can also contact one of the tax companies who can do what is called an investigation. Some charge a very small fee, while other will do it for free.

What Needs to Happen Generally to Setup an Agreement with the IRS or State?

No matter what tax solution tax pros pursue on your behalf with the state or IRS, in most cases, all tax returns both previous and for the current year need to be filed. An attorney or enrolled agent will help prepare and submit tax returns to the IRS (or state) by first gathering all necessary documentation from you, and then by preparing the returns.
