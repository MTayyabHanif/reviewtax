How Do We Determine Rankings for Tax Companies?

We leverage a proprietary algorithm where we take into account many factors including:

Reviews.Tax is a free online resource that strives to provide both business and individual taxpayers with company comparisons and free content. Companies cannot pay for a better position as part of our service. However, we may get compensated by a company when you call or request a quote from our website.

As former tax company owners, we use a proprietary algorithm in order to rank our top ten list using factors such as diversity of tax services, average case fees, BBB ratings, customer reviews, years in business, professionals on staff, refund policies, and more.

One of the founders of reviews.tax is a tax professional and both founders use to own a tax relief company. Our goal is to provide information so you can make an informed decision.

Our Partners: W Tax Group, Tax Defense Network, Community Tax, Justice Tax, Tax Resource Group

1) Diversity of Agencies Serviced - It is always a great thing to work with a tax company that handles both IRS and state tax problems. As form tax firm owners, we know many people who run into problems with the IRS also have state tax problems as well.

2) Average Case Fee - Average case fees printed online are not always accurate. We leveraged a proprietary way to find out what the average cost was to resolve a tax problem with the firms we researched.

3) BBB Rating -Just because someone has a BBB rating doesn't mean they are accredited by the BBB. BBB ratings are a great way to gauge how well a tax firm is doing in providing their services.

4) Reviews: BBB Reviews, Google Reviews, Facebook Reviews & More -We looked at a variety of review sites that we believe are harder to game than someone other websites.

5) Professionals on Staff - It is important to know whether a tax firm has tax professionals on staff or outsources the cases to outside tax professionals.

6) Professionals shown on Website - Anyone can claim to have tax professionals part of their firm but what is their make up? Do they have tax attorneys? CPAs? Enrolled Agents?

7) Years in Business - How long a company is in business was an important factor to us.

8) Number of BBB Complaints - Positive Reviews are less of a key indicator to us. Complaints are a good metric we leverage with our algorithm.
